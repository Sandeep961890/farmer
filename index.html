About Us

Farming & Crops – Complete Guide

1. Introduction to Farming
Farming is the backbone of human civilization, providing food, raw materials, and livelihoods to billions. It involves the cultivation of crops and rearing of animals using soil, water, and technology. Modern farming combines traditional practices with scientific advancements to increase productivity and sustainability.

2. Types of Farming
- Subsistence Farming – Small-scale, mainly for family consumption.
- Commercial Farming – Large-scale farming aimed at selling crops for profit.
- Organic Farming – Uses natural fertilizers and eco-friendly practices, avoids chemicals.
- Mixed Farming – Combines crop cultivation and livestock rearing.
- Agroforestry – Integrates trees with crops for soil and environmental benefits.

3. Major Crops in India (and Globally)
- 🌾 Food Grains: Rice, Wheat, Maize (Corn), Millets.
- 🌱 Commercial Crops: Cotton, Sugarcane, Oilseeds, Tea & Coffee.
- 🥕 Horticulture Crops: Fruits, Vegetables, Spices.

4. Common Problems in Farming
- Climate Change & Weather Risks
- Pests & Diseases
- High Input Costs
- Soil Degradation
- Water Scarcity
- Market Fluctuations
- Small Land Holdings
